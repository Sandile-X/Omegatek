Are Your Smart Devices Putting Your Home at Risk?

Smart homes offer convenience and automation, but they also introduce new security risks. From smart locks to voice assistants, every connected device is a potential entry point for hackers. Hereâ€™s how to keep your home safe.

Common Vulnerabilities

  • Weak or default passwords on devices
  • Unpatched firmware/software
  • Poor network segmentation
  • Unencrypted communications
  • Exposed cloud accounts

How to Protect Your Smart Home

  1. Change Default Passwords: Use strong, unique passwords for every device.
  2. Update Regularly: Keep device firmware and apps up to date.
  3. Secure Your Wi-Fi: Use WPA3 encryption and a separate network for smart devices.
  4. Enable Two-Factor Authentication: Protect cloud accounts and apps.
  5. Monitor Device Activity: Check logs and notifications for unusual behavior.
  6. Disable Unused Features: Turn off services you donâ€™t need.
