Features

Everything You Need at the Gate

Purpose-built for South African conditions "” works on Chainway hardware, reads both driver licences and vehicle discs, stores everything locally.

SA Driver Licence Decode

Reads the encrypted RSA barcode on the back of any South African driver licence card. Extracts name, ID number, licence categories, and expiry instantly.

Vehicle Disc Barcode Read

Scans the NATIS PDF417 barcode on the windscreen licence disc. Captures registration, VIN, engine number, make, and disc expiry date.

IN / OUT Session Logging

Every vehicle gets a paired IN and OUT session with exact timestamps. Active sessions show who is currently on-site. Session duration is calculated automatically.

Automatic Expiry Flagging

Expired driver licences or vehicle discs are automatically flagged with a red badge. Flagged entries appear in the log and are marked in CSV export for easy reporting.

One-Tap CSV Export

Export all session data to a CSV file saved directly to your chosen location "” Downloads, USB drive, or network folder. No cloud upload required.

100% Offline Operation

No internet connection required. All data stays on the device. Works in remote sites, signal-dead zones, or secure environments where connectivity is restricted.

Workflow

From Gate to Report in 3 Steps

The entire flow takes under 15 seconds per vehicle. No typing, no paper forms.

1

Scan the Driver Licence

Point the Chainway scanner at the barcode on the back of the driver licence card. Pull the trigger. The app reads the encrypted data in under a second and displays the driver's name, ID number, licence categories, and expiry date.

2

Scan the Vehicle Disc

Scan the PDF417 barcode on the windscreen licence disc. The app retrieves the vehicle registration number, VIN, engine number, make, and disc expiry date. If no disc is available, tap Skip.

3

Confirm and Log

Review the captured details and tap Confirm. The session is logged with a timestamp. At the end of the shift, tap Export to save a full CSV report to your device.
